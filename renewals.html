About This Retreat

The Spring Renewal is the smaller and more intimate of our two annual retreats. Set in the mountain camp environment, it's a chance to step away from daily life, be in nature, and go deeper with the men of the fellowship.

The format is experiential and evolving — typically including group circles, optional workshops, chanting, shared meals, and unstructured time to connect with other men.

Many men describe the renewals as among the most meaningful experiences of their lives. Going for the first time, not knowing what to expect, and being received by a circle of men — that's the core of it.

A Larger Gathering

The Fall Renewal brings together men's groups from multiple UU churches across Southern California. It's larger, more programmed, and carries the energy of a true men's conference — while still holding the intimacy that defines this tradition.

Camp Stevens in Julian is a beautiful camp at elevation — cool air, tall trees, and a sense of remove from city life. The fall season makes it especially striking.

Programming typically includes opening and closing ceremonies, group circles, workshops, music, chanting, and a talent show or other community gathering on Saturday evening.

First Time?

What to Expect

Whether it's your first renewal or your fifteenth, there's something universal about arriving at camp with a bag and an open heart.

1
You Don't Need to Know Anyone

Many men arrive not knowing what to expect and leave with some of the most meaningful connections of their lives. The renewal is designed to create that. Just show up.

2
There Is No Performance Required

You don't need to be eloquent, emotionally sophisticated, or spiritually advanced. You just need to be willing to be present. The space holds whatever you bring.

3
Bring Comfortable Clothes and an Open Mind

Camp setting — layers, walking shoes, and something to write in if you like. Meals are provided. The structure is loose enough to breathe and intentional enough to hold you.

4
It's Okay to Feel Moved

Men cry at renewals. Men laugh deeply. Men say things they've never said out loud before. That's what the space is for. It's safe.
