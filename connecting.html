It's Simple

How to Join

There's no application, no dues, no membership requirement. Joining is as simple as showing up to a Fourth Monday gathering.

01
Show Up to a Fourth Monday

Our monthly gatherings are open to all men. Potluck dinner starts at 6pm at the Common Room, Hillcrest Campus, First UU San Diego. Come at 7pm for the discussion if you'd rather skip the food for your first time.

02
Introduce Yourself

At the start of the meeting, there's a check-in round. New faces are always welcomed. You'll never be put on the spot — just given the space to say as much or as little as you'd like.

03
Get on the Mailing List

After your first meeting, ask one of the co-conveners to add you to the list. You'll receive monthly reminders about meeting dates, special events, and renewals.

04
Keep Coming Back

Community builds through consistency. The more often you show up, the more the fellowship becomes yours. There's no formal membership — belonging is earned through presence.

Common Questions

FAQ

Is this a religious group?
No religious affiliation is required. UUMF is rooted in Unitarian Universalist principles — inclusive, non-creedal, spiritually open. Men of any faith background (or none) are welcome. We meet at a UU church, but the fellowship itself is a men's community, not a religious one.
Do I need to be a member of First UU San Diego?
No. You don't need to be a church member, a UU member, or have any affiliation with First Unitarian Universalist Church of San Diego. Anyone who identifies as a man is welcome.
Is there a cost to attend?
Monthly Fourth Monday meetings are free. There's no dues or membership fee. Renewal weekends have a modest cost to cover lodging and meals — contact the co-conveners for details. Financial support may be available for those who need it.
Can I attend on Zoom?
Yes. Most Fourth Monday meetings are available via Zoom. Contact one of the co-conveners (see below) to get the Zoom link.
What do I bring to my first meeting?
Just yourself. A dish for the potluck is appreciated but never expected for first-timers. Come at 6pm to share the meal or at 7pm for the discussion — either is fine.
What happens at a meeting?
Potluck dinner at 6pm, then a check-in circle where each man shares briefly how he's doing, then a facilitated discussion on a topic relevant to men's lives. It closes with a chant. Typically done by 9pm. See the Gatherings page for a full breakdown.
What are focus groups?
Focus groups are small, ongoing groups of 5–8 men that meet regularly for deeper sharing and support. They're separate from the monthly meeting and are a long-term commitment. Interest begins at a Fourth Monday — see the Focus Groups page.
