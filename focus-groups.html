Deep Community

Focus Groups

Small groups of five to eight men meeting regularly — built on trust, confidentiality, and long-term commitment.

What They Are

The Heart of Long-Term Community

Focus groups are the heart of long-term community in the fellowship. While the Fourth Monday meetings are the broad circle — welcoming to all men, open and varied — focus groups are where the deepest work happens.


Each group meets regularly at times set by the members themselves, at homes or on Zoom. Five to eight men. No agenda beyond showing up honestly.


Many men join UUMF for the monthly meetings and stay for the focus groups. The depth of relationship that develops over months and years of consistent sharing is unlike most friendships men have access to in everyday life.

01
Small & Intimate

Five to eight men. Small enough that every voice is heard, large enough for diversity of experience.

02
Confidential

What is shared in the group stays in the group. This is the foundation that makes honest sharing possible.

03
Long-Term Commitment

Groups meet regularly over months and years. The depth comes from continuity — from being known over time.

04
Member-Led

Groups set their own schedule, location, and format. Leadership rotates or is shared. No external agenda.

How It Works

A Typical Meeting

Each group finds its own rhythm, but most follow a similar pattern.

🕯️

Opening

A chant, a moment of silence, or a simple centering exercise. Something that marks the transition from the outside world into the space of the group.

🗣️

Check-In

Each man takes time to share where he is — what's alive for him, what he's carrying, what he's celebrating. No cross-talk. Just presence and listening.

🤲

Deeper Work

One or more men might go deeper on something from check-in. The group listens, reflects, and supports — without fixing, advising, or judging.
