ROOTS

Our Home: First UU San Diego

The UUMF meets at First Unitarian Universalist Church of San Diego — one of the oldest congregations in the city. Unitarians first gathered here in 1873, when a small Sunday School met at Horton Hall on June 22 of that year. By 1877, the group had grown and formally organized as The Unitarian Society of San Diego.

The Hillcrest Campus — where the UUMF holds its Fourth Monday gatherings in the Common Room — was built in 1959. The church has been a center for liberal religion, free thought, and social justice in San Diego for over 150 years.

One of the church's founding members, Alonzo Erasmus Horton — the developer whose name still marks a neighborhood downtown — was among San Diego's early Unitarians seeking to establish a center for religious education and free thought.

THE MOUNTAINS

Camp de Benneville Pines

The UUMF's Spring Renewal takes place at Camp de Benneville Pines, a UU camp in the San Bernardino National Forest above Angelus Oaks — at 6,800 feet elevation, surrounded by pine, cedar, and oak. The camp was founded in 1961, purchased by UU congregations from San Diego to Santa Monica to Arizona pooling their resources together.

It remains one of the few UU-affiliated camps in the country and serves over 60 groups a year. For UUMF, it has been the setting for some of the most significant weekends in men's lives — the kind of experience that men return to year after year.

BROADER MOVEMENT

The Southern California UU Men's Network

UUMF is one of several UU men's fellowships in Southern California. The Fall Renewal near Julian brings these groups together for a multi-congregation gathering — men from San Diego to other UU churches across the region spending three days in the mountains together.

The Thread

What Has Endured

Whatever year the fellowship began, and whatever the specific story of its founding, certain things have remained constant: a circle of men showing up for each other, month after month. A table with food on it. A check-in round where every voice is heard. Chants sung together. The mountains, twice a year.

The history is not only in the past. Every man who walks through the door for the first time is adding to it. Every Fourth Monday, every renewal, every focus group conversation — the story keeps going.
