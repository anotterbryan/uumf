Give & Receive

Support the Fellowship

A brotherhood supports itself. Giving back is how we keep the circle whole — from the potluck table to the men who need us most.

Where It Goes

Ways to Give

Your contribution goes directly to the fellowship and the men in it. No overhead. No institution. Just brothers taking care of each other.

🍽️
Potluck & Meeting Fund
Helps cover incidental costs of our monthly Fourth Monday gatherings — supplies, setup, and anything needed to keep the table full and the doors open. Even a few dollars helps offset what members chip in for hosting.
🤝
Members in Need
A reciprocity fund for brothers going through difficult times — unexpected medical bills, job loss, or a moment when they need the circle to hold them financially as well as emotionally. Contributions feed into this pool and are distributed with care and discretion.
🎁
Games & Activities Fund
Gift cards and prizes for games, activities, and special occasions at Fourth Monday gatherings and renewal weekends. A small fund that adds joy to our shared time — and gives men something to take home besides good memories.

In Person

The Donation Jar

A jar is available at each Fourth Monday meeting for cash contributions. It lives at the potluck table. Whatever you can give is appreciated.

By Check

Checks are accepted. Make out to UU Men's Fellowship and bring to a Fourth Monday gathering, or contact a co-convener to arrange delivery.

In Kind

Contributions of food, supplies, gift cards, or time are equally valued. Reach out at a Fourth Monday or through the Reciprocity Ring.

The Spirit of It

Giving as Brotherhood

This is not a charity. It's a circle of mutual care. When you give to the fellowship fund, you're feeding the table you also eat from. When a brother needs support, the circle holds him — and one day, perhaps, he holds someone else.

There are no amounts, no obligations, no expectations. Give what you can, receive what you need. That's the whole model.

The jar at the potluck table is just the physical expression of something deeper — the ongoing commitment to show up for each other, financially as well as emotionally.
