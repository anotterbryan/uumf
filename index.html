Who We Are

Our Mission

Our mission is to support men in the quest for lives of compassion, integrity, responsibility, and balance.


Through mutually supportive work, our fellowship seeks to help men in their development as sons... Fathers... Brothers... Companions and members of society.


We are a caring, intentionally diverse brotherhood, committed to social justice and offering opportunities for intimate sharing, personal growth, and spiritual deepening.

01
Compassion

For ourselves and those around us — without condition or reserve.

02
Integrity

Showing up honestly in all our relationships, especially the hard ones.

03
Responsibility

To our families, our communities, and the wider world we share.

04
Balance

Nurturing our whole selves — mind, body, and spirit.

Gatherings

How We Meet

Three ways to enter the fellowship — each one a different depth of engagement.

🌙

Fourth Mondays

Our primary monthly gathering. Potluck dinner at 6pm — bring a dish to share, or just come as you are if you're new. From 7–9pm we check in and discuss topics relevant to men's lives. Most meetings are available on Zoom as well.

📍 Common Room · Hillcrest Campus
🍽️

February Banquet

A special catered banquet held each February — a celebration of the fellowship and the men in it. A highlight of the year and a wonderful event to bring someone new.

📅 Each February · Details announced at Fourth Monday
🪵

Yule Log Ceremony

A beloved seasonal tradition during the holiday season. The Yule Log ceremony is a time of reflection, ritual, and brotherhood as the year draws to a close.

🕯️ Holiday Season · Date announced at Fourth Monday
🤲

Support Groups

Optional small groups of five to eight men that meet at times agreed upon by the group members — at members' homes or on Zoom. Built on trust, confidentiality, and long-term commitment.

↓ See Support Groups section below
🏕️

Renewal Weekends

Three-day mountain retreats held each Fall and Spring. Fall at Camp Stevens in Julian, Spring at Camp de Benneville Pines. Life-changing events for many men — newcomers especially welcome.

🍂 Fall · 🌸 Spring

Songs and hymns sung at gatherings, renewal weekends, and monthly meetings. Click any title to expand the lyrics.

Hymn #1064
Blue Boat Home
Words: Peter Mayer · Music: Rowland H. Prichard
▶

Though below me, I feel no motion
standing on these mountains and plains
Far away from the rolling ocean
still my dry land heart can say:

I've been sailing all my life now,
never harbor or port have I known
The wide universe is the ocean I travel
and the earth is my blue boat home

Sun my sail and moon my rudder
as I ply the starry sea,
leaning over the edge in wonder,
casting questions into the deep

Drifting here with my ship's companions,
all we kindred pilgrim souls,
making our way by the lights of the heavens
in our beautiful blue boat home

I give thanks to the waves upholding me,
hail the great winds urging me on,
greet the infinite sea before me,
sing the sky my sailor's song:

I was born up on the fathoms,
never harbor or port have I known
The wide universe is the ocean I travel,
and the earth is my blue boat home

Hymn #1055
How Sweet the Darkness
Words: Rachel Bates · Music: Jason Shelton, © 2002
▶

Verse 1:
When windows that are black and cold
are lit anew with fires of gold;
When dusk in quiet shall descend
and darkness come once more a friend.

Verse 2:
When wings pursue their proper flight
and bring not terror but delight;
When clouds are innocent again
and hide no storms of deadly rain;

Verse 3:
And when the sky is swept of wars
and keeps but gentle moon and stars,
that peaceful sky, that harmless air,
how sweet, how sweet the darkness there.

Hymn #1066
O Brother Sun
Adapted from St. Francis of Assisi by Sharon Anway, © 2004 · Music: Traditional Scottish
▶

Verse 1:
O Brother Sun, you bring us light,
all shining 'round in fiery might.
O Sister Moon, you heal and bless,
your beauty shines in tenderness.

Verse 2:
O Brother Fire, you warm our night
with all your dancing colored light.
O Sister Earth, you feed all things,
all birds, all creatures, all scales and wings.

O Brother Wind, you sweep the hills,
your mighty breath both freshens and fills.
O Sister Water, you cleanse and flow
through rivers and streams, in ice and snow.

O Sister Death, you meet us here
and take us to our God so near.
O God of Life, we give you praise
for all your creatures, for all your ways.

Dark of Winter
Words by Shelly Jackson Denham · Used at winter gatherings & renewal weekends
Dark of winter, soft and still, Your quiet calm surrounds me. Let my thoughts go where they will, Ease my mind profoundly And then my soul will sing a song, A blessed song of love eternal. Gentle darkness soft and still Bring your quiet to me. Darkness, soothe my weary eyes, That I may see more clearly. When my heart with sorrow cries, Comfort and caress me. And then my soul may hear a voice, A still small voice of love eternal. Darkness, when my fears arise, Let your peace flow through me.
